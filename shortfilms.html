Drifting

by Inês Frazão

Based on Hannah Arendt's essay We Refugee, the short film Drifting intends to be a reflection on the current refugee condition.
In the thoughts of a young refugee who recently arrived in another country, we get a glimpse of the complex reality of someone who is abruptly removed from their homeland, family, memories and references. We understand how the identity of the one who arrives in a strange land can be altered, or even lost, because of the fear of being different, of being a stranger and of not being accepted.
At the end, a comfort, a note of hope. The narrator tells us that perhaps there is still hope that the lost memories will return and that the diluted identity will finally be recovered.

Miles Away

by Catharina Lima

What is the price of being free far from home? Starting from the principles of the project I do not intend to speak about, I only wish to speak nearby, this audiovisual fiction approaches the feelings of the character through vision, complemented by the poem Things we carry in the Sea by Wang Ping. The use of video and sound is mainly intended to convey the feeling of freedom, questioning and, above all, nostalgia.
In Miles Away, the fiction its based on the feelings of someone who makes the decision to live away from his home country and seeks to reinvent and free himself. The question that is always present is whether, even if it is a voluntary decision, what is the true price of breaking free far from home?

Why Can't Anybody See Me?

by Marta Pascoal

Taking inspiration from the way Agnès Varda filmed The Gleaners and I, Why Can't Anyone See Me? is an extension of the negative feelings felt by marginalized people and others. Specifically focusing on the feeling of loneliness, the highway depicted in the video becomes a purgatory for people who experience it, leaving them disoriented and unable to get around unless they are helped by someone, in this case by hitching a ride with a driver.
