"When you decide to speak nearby, rather than speak about, the first thing you need to do is to acknowledge the possible gap between you and those who populate your film."

- Trinh T. Minh-ha

I do not intend to speak about, I only wish to speak nearby is a fiction based on the movie The Gleaners and I by Agnès Varda. This documentary deals with issues of wealth and poverty in modern day France by exploring the world of gleaners.

Varda explores the narrative with the intention to “glean” memories and events through the use of the DV camera, creating a connection with the act of gleaning and also with the viewers. This involvement as the main vehicle for perception perfectly describes what haptic vision emcompasses. Haptic vision works as the “other side” of perception, delivering knowledge that is felt rather than thought.

This storytelling strategy allows the viewer to come into contact with the elements showcased through their own memories and recollections, instead of using a set of external and pre-established concepts, disrupting any attempt of fetishization and hostility.

We also found that Trinh T. Minh-ha’s film Reassemblage (1982), fully encompasses this understanding of overturning conventions traditionally employed in anthropological filmmaking. Reassemblage is better understood as an anti-ethnography, a film that reflexively dismantles the objectification and exoticization of otherness.

I do not intend to speak about, just speak nearby - in this statement, from which we borrowed the title of our project, Minh-ha refers to her position as a visitor vis-à-vis the villagers she met, and discloses her position as an un-authoritative narrator.

Through our motto, hacking reality, we intend to create a fiction based on current issues. Deriving from Varda’s and Minh-ha's teachings, our project aims to explore how we can build the identity of a person through non verbal queues.

This type of identity formation uses the concept of haptic vision, preventing viewers from coming into contact with the elements with pre-established external notions, and instead allowing them to make contact with the objects through their own memories and experiences stored in their senses, having an active position in the assembly of meaning.

For the project’s teaser we wanted to unveil slowly and subtly the identity of the character that will be featured in our project room. With visual and audio clues we invite any and everyone to try and begin to discover who this person is and what their story is.
